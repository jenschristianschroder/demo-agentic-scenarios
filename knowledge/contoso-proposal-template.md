# Contoso Electronics — Sales Proposal Template & Guidelines

## Company Boilerplate

**Contoso Electronics** is a consumer electronics company founded in 2003, headquartered in Copenhagen, Denmark. We specialize in laptops, tablets, and smart home devices, operating in 14 countries across Europe and North America with approximately 2,800 employees. Our fiscal year 2024 revenue was DKK 4.2 billion (approximately €563 million).

## Proposal Structure

All sales proposals should follow this standard structure:

### 1. Cover Section
- Proposal title: "Sales Proposal — [Customer/Use Case]"
- Date of proposal
- Prepared by: Contoso Electronics Sales Team
- Validity: 30 days from date of proposal

### 2. Executive Summary
A 2–3 sentence overview of the customer's need and the recommended solution. Should mention the recommended product, quantity, and total cost.

### 3. Customer Requirements Summary
Restate the customer's requirements in a structured format:
- Number of devices needed
- Use case / deployment scenario
- Key priorities (e.g., battery life, portability, durability)
- Budget constraints
- Warranty and support requirements
- Any additional notes or constraints

### 4. Recommended Solution
Present the recommended product with full specifications:
- Product name and category
- Display, processor, memory, storage
- Battery life and weight
- Operating system
- Ports and connectivity
- Security features
- Colors available

Include a brief explanation of why this product is the best fit for the customer's stated priorities.

### 5. Pricing Breakdown
Present pricing in a clear table format:
- Unit price (MSRP)
- Volume discount applied (tier and percentage)
- Discounted unit price
- Quantity
- Subtotal
- Any applicable bundle savings
- **Total cost**
- Budget comparison (within/over budget, delta amount)

### 6. Warranty & Support
Summarize the warranty and support coverage:
- Warranty type and duration
- On-site service availability
- Accidental damage protection (if applicable)
- Business support channels and response times
- Warranty extension options available

### 7. Alternative Option
If an alternative product was evaluated, present it briefly:
- Product name and key differentiators
- Price comparison
- Trade-offs vs. the recommended product

### 8. Trade-offs & Considerations
List any trade-offs, concerns, or considerations the customer should be aware of:
- Budget fit issues
- Warranty gaps
- Feature compromises
- Recommendations for mitigation (e.g., warranty extension, accessory additions)

### 9. Recommended Accessories
Suggest relevant accessories based on the deployment scenario:
- Docking stations for desk setups
- Carrying cases for mobile workers
- Spare chargers for field deployments
- Input devices for productivity

### 10. Next Steps & Call to Action
- Invite the customer to proceed with the order
- Provide contact information for the sales team
- Note the proposal validity period (30 days)
- Mention available payment terms (Net 30, leasing options)

## Formatting Guidelines

- Use professional, confident tone — not overly formal
- Use bullet points for specifications and features
- Use bold for product names and key figures
- Include DKK amounts with thousands separators
- Reference specific warranty program names (e.g., "Business On-Site Warranty", "Contoso Business Pro Care")
- Keep the proposal to approximately 400–600 words
- End with a clear call to action
