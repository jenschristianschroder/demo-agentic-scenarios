# Apollo Space Program — Knowledge Base

## Program Overview

The Apollo program was a United States human spaceflight program carried out by the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA). It ran from 1961 to 1972. The program's goal was to land humans on the Moon and return them safely to Earth, fulfilling President John F. Kennedy's national goal set on May 25, 1961.

The total cost of the Apollo program was approximately $25.4 billion (1973 dollars), equivalent to roughly $257 billion in 2024 dollars.

## Apollo Missions

### Apollo 1

- **Date:** January 27, 1967 (launch rehearsal test)
- **Crew:** Gus Grissom (Commander), Ed White (Senior Pilot), Roger B. Chaffee (Pilot)
- **Outcome:** A cabin fire during a launch rehearsal test killed all three crew members. The fire occurred at Launch Complex 34, Cape Kennedy Air Force Station, Florida. This was not a flight mission but a plugs-out test.

### Apollo 7

- **Launch Date:** October 11, 1968
- **Crew:** Wally Schirra (Commander), Donn Eisele (Command Module Pilot), Walter Cunningham (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Duration:** 10 days, 20 hours, 9 minutes, 3 seconds
- **Outcome:** First crewed Apollo mission. Successfully tested the Command/Service Module in Earth orbit. The mission completed 163 orbits.

### Apollo 8

- **Launch Date:** December 21, 1968
- **Crew:** Frank Borman (Commander), Jim Lovell (Command Module Pilot), William Anders (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Duration:** 6 days, 3 hours, 0 minutes, 42 seconds
- **Outcome:** First crewed spacecraft to leave low Earth orbit, reach the Moon, orbit it, and return. The crew completed 10 lunar orbits. William Anders took the famous "Earthrise" photograph on December 24, 1968.

### Apollo 9

- **Launch Date:** March 3, 1969
- **Crew:** James McDivitt (Commander), David Scott (Command Module Pilot), Rusty Schweickart (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Duration:** 10 days, 1 hour, 0 minutes, 54 seconds
- **Outcome:** First crewed flight of the Lunar Module (LM). Tested LM systems in Earth orbit, including docking and undocking procedures. The Lunar Module was nicknamed "Spider" and the Command Module was nicknamed "Gumdrop."

### Apollo 10

- **Launch Date:** May 18, 1969
- **Crew:** Thomas Stafford (Commander), John Young (Command Module Pilot), Eugene Cernan (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Duration:** 8 days, 0 hours, 3 minutes, 23 seconds
- **Outcome:** Dress rehearsal for the Moon landing. The Lunar Module descended to within 15.6 kilometers (8.4 nautical miles) of the lunar surface but did not land. The Lunar Module was nicknamed "Snoopy" and the Command Module was nicknamed "Charlie Brown."

### Apollo 11

- **Launch Date:** July 16, 1969
- **Crew:** Neil Armstrong (Commander), Michael Collins (Command Module Pilot), Buzz Aldrin (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Landing Date:** July 20, 1969
- **Landing Site:** Sea of Tranquility (Mare Tranquillitatis)
- **Duration:** 8 days, 3 hours, 18 minutes, 35 seconds
- **EVA Duration:** 2 hours, 31 minutes, 40 seconds
- **Outcome:** First crewed Moon landing. Neil Armstrong was the first person to walk on the Moon at 02:56 UTC on July 21, 1969. His first words on the lunar surface were "That's one small step for [a] man, one giant leap for mankind." Armstrong and Aldrin spent 21 hours and 36 minutes on the lunar surface. They collected 21.55 kilograms (47.51 pounds) of lunar material.

### Apollo 12

- **Launch Date:** November 14, 1969
- **Crew:** Charles "Pete" Conrad (Commander), Richard Gordon (Command Module Pilot), Alan Bean (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Landing Date:** November 19, 1969
- **Landing Site:** Ocean of Storms (Oceanus Procellarum)
- **Duration:** 10 days, 4 hours, 36 minutes, 24 seconds
- **Outcome:** Second crewed Moon landing. The spacecraft was struck by lightning twice during launch (at 36.5 seconds and 52 seconds after liftoff). The crew landed within walking distance of the Surveyor 3 unmanned probe, which had landed on April 20, 1967. They retrieved parts of Surveyor 3 and collected 34.35 kilograms (75.73 pounds) of lunar samples.

### Apollo 13

- **Launch Date:** April 11, 1970
- **Crew:** Jim Lovell (Commander), Jack Swigert (Command Module Pilot), Fred Haise (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Duration:** 5 days, 22 hours, 54 minutes, 41 seconds
- **Outcome:** The mission was aborted after an oxygen tank in the Service Module exploded on April 13, 1970, approximately 321,860 kilometers from Earth. The crew used the Lunar Module "Aquarius" as a lifeboat and performed a free-return trajectory around the Moon. Jack Swigert replaced the original Command Module Pilot, Ken Mattingly, who was grounded due to exposure to measles. The crew splashed down safely in the South Pacific on April 17, 1970.

### Apollo 14

- **Launch Date:** January 31, 1971
- **Crew:** Alan Shepard (Commander), Stuart Roosa (Command Module Pilot), Edgar Mitchell (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Landing Date:** February 5, 1971
- **Landing Site:** Fra Mauro formation
- **Duration:** 9 days, 0 hours, 1 minute, 58 seconds
- **Outcome:** Third crewed Moon landing. Alan Shepard, at age 47, was the oldest person to walk on the Moon during the Apollo program. He famously hit two golf balls on the lunar surface. The crew collected 42.28 kilograms (93.21 pounds) of lunar samples.

### Apollo 15

- **Launch Date:** July 26, 1971
- **Crew:** David Scott (Commander), Alfred Worden (Command Module Pilot), James Irwin (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Landing Date:** July 30, 1971
- **Landing Site:** Hadley Rille / Apennine Mountains
- **Duration:** 12 days, 7 hours, 11 minutes, 53 seconds
- **Outcome:** Fourth crewed Moon landing and first "J mission" (extended stay). First mission to use the Lunar Roving Vehicle (LRV). The crew spent 18 hours and 35 minutes on three EVAs. They collected 77.31 kilograms (170.44 pounds) of lunar samples, including the "Genesis Rock" (sample 15415), an anorthosite estimated to be 4.1 billion years old.

### Apollo 16

- **Launch Date:** April 16, 1972
- **Crew:** John Young (Commander), Ken Mattingly (Command Module Pilot), Charles Duke (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Landing Date:** April 21, 1972
- **Landing Site:** Descartes Highlands
- **Duration:** 11 days, 1 hour, 51 minutes, 5 seconds
- **Outcome:** Fifth crewed Moon landing. The crew used the Lunar Roving Vehicle and conducted three EVAs totaling 20 hours and 14 minutes. They collected 95.71 kilograms (211.00 pounds) of lunar samples. Charles Duke, at age 36, was the youngest person to walk on the Moon.

### Apollo 17

- **Launch Date:** December 7, 1972
- **Crew:** Eugene Cernan (Commander), Ronald Evans (Command Module Pilot), Harrison Schmitt (Lunar Module Pilot)
- **Landing Date:** December 11, 1972
- **Landing Site:** Taurus-Littrow Valley
- **Duration:** 12 days, 13 hours, 51 minutes, 59 seconds
- **Outcome:** Sixth and final crewed Moon landing. This was the last Apollo mission and the last time humans traveled beyond low Earth orbit. Harrison Schmitt was the only professional geologist to walk on the Moon. The crew collected 110.52 kilograms (243.65 pounds) of lunar samples — the largest amount of any Apollo mission. Eugene Cernan was the last person to walk on the Moon; his final words on the surface were: "We leave as we came and, God willing, as we shall return, with peace and hope for all mankind."

## Saturn V Launch Vehicle

The Saturn V was the rocket used to launch Apollo missions to the Moon. It was designed by Wernher von Braun and the Marshall Space Flight Center.

- **Height:** 110.6 meters (363 feet)
- **Diameter:** 10.1 meters (33 feet)
- **Mass at launch:** 2,970,000 kilograms (6,540,000 pounds)
- **Stages:** 3 (S-IC, S-II, S-IVB)
- **Total launches:** 13 (all successful)
- **First flight:** November 9, 1967 (Apollo 4, uncrewed)
- **Manufacturer:** Boeing (S-IC), North American Aviation (S-II), Douglas Aircraft (S-IVB)
